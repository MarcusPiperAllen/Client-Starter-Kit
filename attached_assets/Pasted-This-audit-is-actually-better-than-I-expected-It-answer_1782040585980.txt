This audit is actually better than I expected. It answered the question we were really trying to answer:

> "Is LaunchFrame ready to become a real product, or is it still just a personal tool?"

The answer is:

**It is a real product foundation, but it's still operating as a template engine instead of a planning engine.** 

---

# What I would NOT do next

I would **not**:

* Add voice notes
* Add transcript parsing
* Add AI
* Add PDFs
* Add Notion export
* Add competitor analysis

Those are all downstream features.

The audit found a more important problem:

### The engine is lying

The SaaS test exposed it.

The user described a SaaS company.

LaunchFrame generated food business content. 

That means the app is not actually understanding project types.

It is selecting templates.

Until that is fixed, every new feature sits on a shaky foundation.

---

# What I would do next

I would declare:

## LaunchFrame Phase 2

### "Project Intelligence"

Goal:

> Make the generated output correct before making it smarter.
